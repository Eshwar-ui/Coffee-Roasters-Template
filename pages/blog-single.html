Dialing in the Perfect Espresso Shot at Home

Marcus Vance

Head Roaster

Turning on your home espresso machine and hoping for the best rarely yields the syrupy, balanced shots you get at a specialty café. True consistency comes from a process called "dialing in."

The Golden Ratio

Before adjusting anything on your grinder, you need a target recipe. The industry standard "Golden Ratio" for modern espresso is 1:2. This means for every 1 gram of ground coffee you put into the portafilter (dose), you want to extract 2 grams of liquid espresso (yield).

  • Dose: 18 grams (typical double basket)
  • Yield: 36 grams of liquid espresso
  • Time: 25-30 seconds
"The grinder—not the espresso machine—is the single most important tool in dialing in coffee."

The Variable: Adjusting the Grind

Once you have your dose locked in at exactly 18.0g, your only variable to change the extraction time is the grind size.

Troubleshooting Grind Size:

Shot runs too fast (Under 20s): The water is finding too little resistance. The coffee tastes sour and thin. Solution: Grind Finer.

Shot runs too slow (Over 35s): The water is struggling to push through the puck. The coffee tastes bitter and harsh. Solution: Grind Coarser.

Putting it Together

You will need a scale that measures to 0.1g accuracy. Tare your cup on the scale beneath the portafilter. Start the shot and the timer simultaneously. Stop the shot right as the scale hits 36g. Look at your timer. Is it at 28 seconds? Congratulations, you are dialed in.
